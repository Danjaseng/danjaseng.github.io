Rainfeeder

Rainfeeders are relatively small yet highly adaptable creatures commonly found in areas with high precipitation. They are docile and avoid confrontation with other creatures. They are characterized by a beak on the top of their heads. During rainfall, the beak opens to feed upon the water.

Rainfeeders have a specialized flagella used for traversal over ground. The flagella functions as a sort of spring or pogo stick that allows them to move in a bouncing motion. Rainfeeders also have a single eye, giving the ability to see. It has clear vision but lacks the depth perception that multiple-eyed creatures posess. They use their eyesight to find suitable nesting grounds (often under trees) or to locate hostile predators. After spotting a predator, rainfeeders will desperately attempt to hop away. However, they lack any defensive capability after being caught, at best resorting to a helpless flail.

Rainfeeders have a multitude of activities to pass the time:

  • Hopping
  • Looking at things
  • Feeding on rain
  • Listening to music
  • And other things too!
